This article is about the tangent "planes" of an algebraic variety, in affine and projective spaces. We assume the reader is familiar with the definition of regularity, smoothness, the Zariski tangent space, and the Jacobian criterion. For a more general discussion of Jacobian criteria, see This Page.

Tangents Planes in Affine Spaces

Suppose a scheme $X$ is a closed subscheme of an affine space $\mathbb{A}^n_k$ cutted out by an ideal $$I=(f_1,\ldots,f_r)$$ and $X$ is smooth of pure dimension $d$ at a $k$-valued point $a=(a_1,\ldots,a_n)$, then the tangent $d$-plane to $X$ at $a$ (denoted as $T_a X$) is defined by the linear equations $$ \sum_{j=1}^n{ \left(\frac{\partial f_i}{\partial x_j}(a)\right)(x_j-a_j) } =0, \quad i=1,\ldots,r. $$ Where $\frac{\partial f_i}{\partial x_j}(a)$ is the partial derivative of $f_i$ with respect to $x_j$ evaluated at $a$. Note that the tangent plane is well-defined, i.e., independent of the choice of generators of $I$, and it is a linear subscheme of $\mathbb{A}^n_k$ of dimension $d$.
